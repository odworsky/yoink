What Are Evolutionary Algorithms?

Evolutionary algorithms are used to develop AI for a variety of different scenarios. The idea is modeled off of the mechanisms of actual evolution in biology. Developers initialize a random population with a variety of relevant attributes, and determine which models have the greatest fitness.

Terminology

"A population is a set of candidates for the solution, and its representation is called genotype. A generation is a step in the process where evolutionary operations take place on the population."

Trackmania AI

To teach AI to race efficiently, a population is created of different AI neural networks. The population is then put to the test to determine fitness. This 'fitness' is specific to the task, in this case racing the furthest and the fastest across the course. AI Learns to Escape (Deep
Reinforcement Learning)

While not technically an evolutionary algorithm, the coder uses deep reinforcement learning to apply the same theory to individuals rather than an entire population. Every generation is only one iteration of a neural network, rewarded for having greater fitness. The next generation is modeled off of the last until the task is accomplished.
